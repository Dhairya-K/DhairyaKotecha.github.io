About Me

I am deeply interested in astrophysics at the junction of theoretical and observational work, where physical models can be built and tested using real data. I want to make my own theories, code my own models, and test them with observations — and also work the other way around, starting from observational data and developing theories to understand what we see.

I am particularly drawn to extreme environments such as supernovae, black holes, neutron stars, pulsars, and the early universe, where fundamental physics is pushed to its limits. I am also fascinated by telescopes, their engineering, data collection, and how we decode the universe. I am also interested in the search for exoplanets and exomoons, and in figuring out dark matter and the origin of our universe. I am still relatively new to the field and would love to participate in all of astrophysics and grow my interest further.

My experience at the Radio Astronomy Winter School at IUCAA confirmed my desire to pursue research in astrophysics. The school helped me work with real data and understand how theory, observation, and instrumentation come together.
